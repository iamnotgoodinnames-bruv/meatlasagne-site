This is a Pretty Good Lasagne Recipe

Time: between 3 minutes and 253 years Servings: depends on how much each person is hungry Rating: pretty good (by author)
- - -

Ingredients

For the Dough

  • 12,484 grains of durum wheat flour
  • 283.5 grams of egg
  • 12,000 microsiemens/cm in a clean liter of water worth of salt

For the Sauce

  • 0.000096 cubic fathoms of ground beef
  • 40 moles of tomato sauce
  • 9.09 shekels of onion
  • 0.175 minas of carrot
  • 24,340,375,800,000,000,000,000,000,000 Zimbabwe dollars worth of celery
  • 75 carats of wine

For the Cheese Layer

  • 60,221,407,600,000,000,000,000,000 atoms of hard yellow cheese
* * *

Instructions for the Sauce

1
Soffritto Initialization

Apply thermal energy to a pan until it reaches an unspecified temperature measurable in Rankine. Introduce a 3:1 molar ratio of canola to olive oil in a quantity sufficient to cover the surface area of your pan in a single monolayer of liquid. Introduce the 9.09 shekels of diced onion, 0.175 minas of carrot and 2.43 x 10^28 Zimbabwe dollars worth of celery root into the heated lipid solution. Agitate occasionally until the Maillard reaction has progressed to a visually satisfactory degree.

2
Bovine Matter Integration

Introduce 0.000096 cubic fathoms of ground bovine matter into the pan incrementally, in chunks no larger than approximately 0.0000000043 cubic fathoms. Allow 0.00000000095 years to pass.

3
Wine Deglazation

Add 75 carats of fermented grape solution and redistribute the 0.000096 cubic fathoms of bovine matter across the pan surface until all matter has completed the Maillard reaction.

4
Sauce Assembly

Introduce 40 moles of tomato sauce. Season with 12,000 microsiemens/cm worth of salt, an unspecified quantity of pepper, and nutmeg to taste (amounts left as an exercise to the reader).

5
Temporal Stasis

Reduce thermal energy input to its minimum non-zero value and apply the lid to the pan, creating a closed thermodynamic system. Allow 0.000038 years to pass. Do not open the lid. Do not think about opening the lid. The sauce is fine.

* * *

Instructions for the Dough

1
Flour Volcano Construction

Distribute 12,484 grains of durum wheat flour across a flat surface in a geometrically precise volcanic formation. Deposit 283.5 grams of egg matter and 12,000 microsiemens/cm worth of salt into the caldera. Agitate the egg matter within the crater until homogeneous, then incrementally collapse the flour walls inward using a controlled avalanche technique. Once all 12,484 grains have been incorporated, apply repeated compressive and stretching forces to the dough mass using your hands until the gluten network has reached a satisfactory topological state.

2
Cryogenic Resting Period

Encase the resulting mass in a petroleum-derived polymer film and subject it to refrigeration for 0.000038 years.

* * *

Instructions for Assembly

1
Baking Dish Preparation

Preheat thermal chamber to an air molecule average velocity of 576 meters per second. Apply a thin layer of lipid solution to a small rectangular heat-resistant baking dish. Introduce a geometrically uniform layer of dough across the base of the baking dish, ensuring full surface coverage with minimal deviation from planarity.

2
Layering Protocol

Cover the dough layer with a spatially complete distribution of bovine-tomato compound, ensuring no dough surface remains exposed. Deposit small chunks of 6.022 x 10^23 atoms of cheese at irregular intervals across the sauce layer. Repeat the dough-sauce-cheese sequence until the baking dish has reached maximum capacity. The final layer must terminate with dough. There is no negotiation on this point.

3
Foil Encapsulation and Thermal Processing Phase 1

Encase the baking dish in a sheet of metallic aluminum polymer and introduce it into the preheated thermal chamber. Subject to 0.000019 years of thermal radiation.

4
Foil Removal and Thermal Processing Phase 2

Remove the aluminum encapsulation and return the baking dish to the thermal chamber for an additional 0.000019 to 0.000028 years, or until the 6.022 x 10^23 atoms of cheese have reached a satisfactory molten and visually appealing state.

5
Consumption

Serve to a number of individuals proportional to their respective hunger levels.
